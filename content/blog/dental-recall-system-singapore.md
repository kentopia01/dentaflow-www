# The Dental Recall System That Brings Patients Back Automatically

Most dental patients don't switch clinics because they're unhappy. They drift. Life gets busy. The 6-month check-up reminder doesn't come, the visit gets postponed, and before long it's been 18 months since they last saw a dentist.

For Singapore dental clinics, this represents a significant untapped revenue stream. A patient who visited 12 months ago and hasn't returned is likely to respond positively to a well-timed, personal-feeling recall message.

## What is Dental Recall?

Dental recall is the process of proactively contacting patients who are overdue for a visit — typically 6 to 12 months after their last appointment.

Traditional recall involves a receptionist working through a patient list and calling each one. In a busy clinic, this rarely happens systematically. The calls take time, most go unanswered, and the return on effort feels low.

Automated recall reverses the economics: one setup, then the system runs continuously.

## The 6-Month and 12-Month Windows

Patients should be contacted at two points:

**6 months post-visit** — for regular check-up and scaling patients
The recall message should reference the treatment: "It's been 6 months since your last scaling at [Clinic]. Time to keep that smile in great shape — book your next appointment below."

**12 months post-visit** — for patients who missed the 6-month recall or whose treatments don't require biannual visits
A gentle nudge: "It's been a while since we've seen you at [Clinic]. Your dental health matters — let's get you back in."

Avoid messaging patients at both points if they've already rebooked.

## Recall Message Effectiveness on WhatsApp

WhatsApp recall campaigns consistently outperform SMS and email for Singapore clinics. The open rates are higher, the reply rates are higher, and patients expect business communications via WhatsApp.

The key elements of an effective recall message:

1. **Personal reference** — mention the clinic name, ideally the last treatment
2. **Clear ask** — link directly to the booking page, not the clinic homepage
3. **Low pressure** — this is a helpful reminder, not a sales pitch
4. **Easy opt-out** — reply STOP to stop, as required by WhatsApp Business policies

## Segmenting Your Recall Queue

Not all patients should receive the same recall message. Basic segmentation:

- **Last treatment was routine (scaling, check-up)** — 6-month recall
- **Last treatment was restorative (filling, crown)** — 12-month recall + "How has your [treatment] been?"
- **Patient has anxiety notes on file** — softer message, consider mentioning your calm environment
- **Patient has children** — if they booked for kids previously, a family recall can work well

Most clinic software stores treatment history. A basic export and filter is all that's needed to segment by last treatment type.

## Building the Recall Pipeline

A simple recall workflow:

1. At the end of each day, identify appointments marked as completed
2. Set a recall date: 6 months later (or 12 months for restorative)
3. Queue the recall message to send on that date
4. When the message goes out, route any replies to a staff member for follow-up booking

With automation, steps 2-4 happen without staff involvement.

## What to Do When Patients Respond

A recall campaign that generates responses needs a fast reply path. The worst outcome: a patient responds "yes, I'd like to book" and waits 4 hours for a reply.

The solution: route recall responses directly to a staff member who can send a booking link immediately. Some platforms allow patients to self-book directly from the recall message — eliminating the human step entirely.

## Measuring Recall ROI

Track per batch:
- Messages sent
- Open/read rate
- Replies
- Bookings generated (from a unique tracking link)
- Revenue from recalled patients in the following 30 days

A typical Singapore dental recall campaign with good segmentation generates 8-15% booking conversion — meaning 1 in 8 to 1 in 7 patients contacted books an appointment. At $150-$250 average treatment value, the maths on a quarterly recall campaign of 200 patients is significant.
